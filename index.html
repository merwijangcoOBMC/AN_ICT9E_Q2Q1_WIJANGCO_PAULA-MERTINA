BITCOIN

Bitcoin (BTC) is the first and most well-known cryptocurrency, introduced in 2008 by an anonymous individual or group using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. It is a decentralized digital currency that allows for peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries like banks or governments. Bitcoin's design is based on blockchain technology, a distributed ledger system that ensures security, transparency, and immutability. ETHEREUM

Ethereum is a decentralized, open-source blockchain platform that enables developers to build and deploy smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps). It was proposed by Vitalik Buterin in late 2013 and went live in July 2015. Ethereum is the second-largest cryptocurrency by market capitalization, after Bitcoin, but it differs from Bitcoin in both functionality and design. While Bitcoin primarily serves as a store of value and a digital currency, Ethereum is designed to be a platform for decentralized applications and smart contract execution.

RIPPLE

Ripple (XRP) is both a digital payment protocol and a cryptocurrency designed to facilitate fast, low-cost cross-border money transfers. Ripple was created by the company Ripple Labs in 2012, with the aim of addressing inefficiencies in the global payment system by offering a faster and more cost-effective way to transfer money across borders. The Ripple network is unique because it operates differently from most cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum. While many blockchains are designed to be fully decentralized, Ripple is partially centralized and focuses on facilitating interbank transactions and remittances through a network of trusted financial institutions..
